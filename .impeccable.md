## Design Context

### Users
Board game enthusiasts across three modes of use:
- **At the table (mobile)**: Logging scores mid-session or immediately after; quick inputs, glanceable results, forgiving of ambient lighting
- **Post-session (desktop)**: Digging into stats, comparing performance, reviewing tribe history; comfortable with data density
- **Group organizers (any device)**: Managing tribes, tracking standings, overseeing community health

The product spans casual friend groups to serious competitive circles. Design must work for both — it shouldn't feel too casual for the numbers people or too intimidating for newcomers.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Community, Nerdy, Interactive

- **Community** → Trakka is not a solo stats tracker. It's about the group. The tribe. Who won game night. Design should surface other people — faces, names, rankings — as prominently as raw data.
- **Nerdy** → Users love the numbers. Don't hide stats behind progressive disclosure; reward the data-hungry. Information density is a feature, not a problem.
- **Interactive** → The app should feel alive. Standings change. Streaks are threatened. Comparisons should feel like challenges. The interface has stakes.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Primary references**:
- **Strava**: Social layer over personal performance. Activity feeds, kudos, segment comparisons, group leaderboards. Community achievements feel earned, not decorative.
- **Yahoo Finance**: Data-forward without being cold. Dense tables that are readable. Numbers are the hero — not hidden behind cards, but front and center.
- **Sports apps** (ESPN, NBA, Premier League): Bold type hierarchy for standings and scores. Competitive energy. Dark backgrounds make stats pop. Broadcast-style confidence.
- **Board Game Arena**: Platform-native for competitive tabletop gaming. Game imagery, player avatars, match history.

**Theme**: Dark-first. Game nights happen in living rooms at 9pm. Sports dashboards go dark. Stats on dark backgrounds pop. Both light and dark are supported in the codebase — but dark is the primary experience to design toward.

**Color palette direction**:
- Deep blue primary (~262° OKLCH) is established and strong — keep it
- The logo's three bar colors (green ~156°, teal ~203°, amber/yellow) are underused in the UI — they should appear as meaningful accent signals (wins, performance indicators, tier badges), not decoration
- Neutrals should be tinted toward the blue brand hue (already done well in globals.css)
- Avoid adding new colors; lean into the existing spectrum more intentionally

**Typography direction**:
- **Display / headings**: Barlow Condensed (available on Google Fonts). Sports broadcast energy. Works at large sizes for standings, scores, game names. Condensed variants let you pack information without it feeling cramped. Pairs the "nerdy + competitive" brief as a *sports scoreboard font*.
- **Body / UI text**: Figtree (Google Fonts). Humanist, slightly warm, readable at small sizes. Not on the banned list. Has the community warmth that offsets Barlow's broadcast rigidity.
- The existing **Asimovian** is reserved for brand moments (logo, hero wordmarks) — it's too stylized for dense UI text.
- **Lora** (current body font) is on the reflex-reject list and reads too editorial/literary for a competitive stats platform. Phase out in favor of Figtree.

### Design Principles

1. **Numbers first, chrome second** — Stats, scores, and rankings should be the most visually prominent elements on any screen. Don't bury them in card padding or muted text. If a number matters, it should look like it matters.

2. **The tribe is the product** — Every screen should remind users they're part of a group. Player avatars, names, and relative standings should appear earlier and more often than generic labels. "You vs. the tribe" is the core emotional hook.

3. **Competitive energy without aggression** — The interface should feel like the moment before a game starts: focused, a little tense, but fun. Not cold. Not sterile. Use color purposefully to signal outcomes (green = win, destructive = loss) without over-dramatizing.

4. **Density is a feature** — The Yahoo Finance reference is intentional. Users who want to dig can dig. Tables can have many rows. Stats can stack. Don't flatten information just to look minimal. Use hierarchy (size, weight, contrast) to make density navigable.

5. **Dark, alive, and grounded** — The primary experience is dark mode, in a living room, on a phone or laptop. Backgrounds should feel rich, not flat. Accents should be deliberate. Motion should signal state changes (new session logged, position changed in standings), not decorate.

### Anti-References
- **Notion / Linear**: Too flat, too pastel, too "productivity tool" — Trakka should feel more like a sports platform than a workspace
- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: Icon + heading + text card grids, hero metric templates, gradient text headlines — all already present in the codebase and should be phased out
- **Mobile games (candy/casual)**: Not playful or cartoonish — the nerdy user base takes their stats seriously
